The 6 Degrees of Separation

The six degrees of separation is the theory that every person on the planet is connected to any other person through a chain of no more than five intermediaries. First proposed in 1929 by Frigyes Karinthy in a short story called "Chains," the theory was tested and supported by Stanley Milgram in something he called the "Small World Problem."

In 2008, Microsoft validated the experiment by analyzing the minimum chain length required to connect 180 million different pairs of users with accounts. They found the average chain to be 6.6 hops. Since then, Facebook claims to have reduced 6.6 to 3.5 degrees through their vast and interconnected network.
